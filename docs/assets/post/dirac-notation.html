Direc Notation

Direc notation is used to describe quantum states:

Let \(a, b \in \mathbb{C}^{2}\)

  • ket: \( \left| a \right\rangle = \left( \begin{array}{c} a_{1} \\ a_{2} \end{array} \right) \)
  • bra: \( \left\langle b \right| = \left| b \right\rangle^{\dagger} = \left( \begin{array}{c} b_1 \\ b_2 \end{array} \right)^{\dagger} = \left( b_{1}^{*}, b_{2}^{*} \right) \)
  • bra-ket: \( \left\langle b | a \right\rangle = a_{1}b_{1}^{*} + a_{2}b_{2}^{*} = \left\langle a | b \right\rangle^{*} \in \mathbb{C} \)
  • ket-bra: \( \left| a \right\rangle \left\langle b \right| = \left( \begin{array}{cc} a_{1}b_{1}^{*} & a_{1}b_{2}^{*} \\ a_{2}b_{1}^{*} & a_{2}b_{2}^{*} \end{array}\right) \)

where \( \left| b \right\rangle^{\dagger} \) denotes the vector with complex conjugated and transposed entries.

We define the states \( \left| 0 \right\rangle = \left( \begin{array}{c} 1 \\ 0 \end{array} \right) \) and \( \left| 1 \right\rangle = \left( \begin{array}{c} 0 \\ 1 \end{array} \right) \), which are orthogonal: \( \left\langle 0 | 1 \right\rangle = 1 \cdot 0 + 0 \cdot 1 = 0. \)

All quantum states are normalized, i.e., \( \left\langle \psi | \psi \right\rangle = 1 \), e.g., \( \left| \psi \right\rangle = \frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}\left( \left| 0 \right\rangle+ \left| 1 \right\rangle \right) = \left(\begin{array}{c} \frac{1}{\sqrt{2}} \\ \frac{1}{\sqrt{2}} \end{array} \right). \)
