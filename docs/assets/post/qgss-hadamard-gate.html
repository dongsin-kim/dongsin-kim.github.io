Single qubit gates

Quantum Circuits - Hadamard Gate

Hadamard gate \(H\) is one of the most important gates for quantum circuits. $$\begin{align} H &=\frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}\left( \left| 0 \right\rangle \left\langle 0 \right| + \left| 0 \right\rangle \left\langle 1 \right| + \left| 1 \right\rangle \left\langle 0 \right| - \left| 1 \right\rangle \left\langle 1 \right| \right) \\ &= \left( \begin{array}{cc} 1 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 \end{array}\right) + \left( \begin{array}{cc} 0 & 1 \\ 0 & 0 \end{array}\right) + \left( \begin{array}{cc} 0 & 0 \\ 1 & 0 \end{array}\right) + \left( \begin{array}{cc} 0 & 0 \\ 0 & -1 \end{array}\right) \\ &= \frac{1}{\sqrt{2}} \left( \begin{array}{cc} 1 & 1 \\ 1 & -1 \end{array}\right) \end{align}$$

Because it creates a superposition and is applied to change from \(z\) to \(x\)-basis. $$\begin{align} H \left| 0 \right\rangle &= \frac{1}{\sqrt{2}} \left( \begin{array}{cc} 1 & 1 \\ 1 & -1 \end{array}\right) \left( \begin{array}{c} 1 \\ 0 \end{array}\right) = \frac{1}{\sqrt{2}} \left( \begin{array}{c} 1 \\ 1 \end{array}\right) = \frac{1}{\sqrt{2}} \left( \left| 0 \right\rangle + \left| 1 \right\rangle \right) = \left| + \right\rangle \\ H \left| 1 \right\rangle &= \frac{1}{\sqrt{2}} \left( \begin{array}{cc} 1 & 1 \\ 1 & -1 \end{array}\right) \left( \begin{array}{c} 0 \\ 1 \end{array}\right) = \frac{1}{\sqrt{2}} \left( \begin{array}{c} 1 \\ -1 \end{array}\right) = \frac{1}{\sqrt{2}} \left( \left| 0 \right\rangle - \left| 1 \right\rangle \right) = \left| - \right\rangle \end{align}$$

In this case, it used to change from \(x\) to \(z\)-basis. $$\begin{align} H \left| + \right\rangle &= \frac{1}{\sqrt{2}} \left( \begin{array}{cc} 1 & 1 \\ 1 & -1 \end{array}\right) \frac{1}{\sqrt{2}} \left( \begin{array}{c} 1 \\ 1 \end{array}\right) = \left( \begin{array}{c} 1 \\ 0 \end{array}\right) = \left| 0 \right\rangle \\ H \left| - \right\rangle &= \frac{1}{\sqrt{2}} \left( \begin{array}{cc} 1 & 1 \\ 1 & -1 \end{array}\right) \frac{1}{\sqrt{2}} \left( \begin{array}{c} 1 \\ -1 \end{array}\right) = \left( \begin{array}{c} 0 \\ 1 \end{array}\right) = \left| 1 \right\rangle \end{align}$$

Similary, as the Phase gate \( S = \left( \begin{array}{cc} 1 & 0 \\ 0 & i \end{array} \right) \) makes a rotation \(\left(\varphi\right)\) around \(z\)-axis by \(\pi / 2 \),
\(S \cdot H\) is applied to change from \(z\) to \(y\)-basis. $$\begin{align} S \cdot H \left| 0 \right\rangle &= S \left| + \right\rangle = \left( \begin{array}{cc} 1 & 0 \\ 0 & i \end{array}\right) \frac{1}{\sqrt{2}} \left( \begin{array}{c} 1 \\ 1 \end{array}\right) = \frac{1}{\sqrt{2}} \left( \begin{array}{c} 1 \\ i \end{array}\right) = \frac{1}{\sqrt{2}} \left( \left| 0 \right\rangle + i \left| 1 \right\rangle \right) = \left| +i \right\rangle \\ S \cdot H \left| 1 \right\rangle &= S \left| - \right\rangle = \left( \begin{array}{cc} 1 & 0 \\ 0 & i \end{array}\right) \frac{1}{\sqrt{2}} \left( \begin{array}{c} 1 \\ -1 \end{array}\right) = \frac{1}{\sqrt{2}} \left( \begin{array}{c} 1 \\ -i \end{array}\right) = \frac{1}{\sqrt{2}} \left( \left| 0 \right\rangle - i \left| 1 \right\rangle \right) = \left| -i \right\rangle\end{align}$$

Please note that:

  • \( \left\{ \left| 0 \right\rangle , \left| 1 \right\rangle \right\} \): \(z\)-basis,
  • \( \left\{ \left| + \right\rangle, \left| - \right\rangle\right\} \): \(x\)-basis,
  • \( \left\{ \left| +i \right\rangle, \left| -i \right\rangle\right\} \): \(y\)-basis.
