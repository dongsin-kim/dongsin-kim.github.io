Single qubit gates

Quantum Circuits - Pauli Gates

Pauli X-gate \(\sigma_{x}\) is a bit-flip gate. $$\begin{align} \sigma_{x} &=\left| 0 \right\rangle \left\langle 1 \right| + \left| 1 \right\rangle \left\langle 0 \right| \\ &= \left( \begin{array}{c} 1 \\ 0 \end{array}\right) \left( \begin{array}{cc} 0 & 1 \end{array}\right) + \left( \begin{array}{c} 0 \\ 1 \end{array}\right) \left( \begin{array}{cc} 1 & 0 \end{array}\right) \\ &= \left( \begin{array}{cc} 0 & 1 \\ 0 & 0 \end{array} \right) + \left( \begin{array}{cc} 0 & 0 \\ 1 & 0 \end{array} \right) \\ &= \left( \begin{array}{cc} 0 & 1 \\ 1 & 0 \end{array}\right) \end{align}$$

$$\begin{align} \sigma_{x} \left| 0 \right\rangle &= \left( \begin{array}{cc} 0 & 1 \\ 1 & 0 \end{array}\right) \left( \begin{array}{c} 1 \\ 0 \end{array}\right) = \left( \begin{array}{c} 0 \\ 1 \end{array}\right) = \left| 1 \right\rangle \\ \sigma_{x} \left| 1 \right\rangle &= \left( \begin{array}{cc} 0 & 1 \\ 1 & 0 \end{array}\right) \left( \begin{array}{c} 0 \\ 1 \end{array}\right) = \left( \begin{array}{c} 1 \\ 0 \end{array}\right) = \left| 0 \right\rangle \end{align}$$

Pauli Z-gate \(\sigma_{z}\) is a phase-flip gate. $$\begin{align} \sigma_{z} &=\left| 0 \right\rangle \left\langle 0 \right| - \left| 1 \right\rangle \left\langle 1 \right| \\ &= \left( \begin{array}{c} 1 \\ 0 \end{array}\right) \left( \begin{array}{cc} 1 & 0 \end{array}\right) - \left( \begin{array}{c} 0 \\ 1 \end{array}\right) \left( \begin{array}{cc} 0 & 1 \end{array}\right) \\ &= \left( \begin{array}{cc} 1 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 \end{array} \right) - \left( \begin{array}{cc} 0 & 0 \\ 0 & 1 \end{array} \right) \\ &= \left( \begin{array}{cc} 1 & 0 \\ 0 & -1 \end{array}\right) \end{align}$$

$$\begin{align} \sigma_{z} \left| + \right\rangle &= \left( \begin{array}{cc} 1 & 0 \\ 0 & -1 \end{array}\right) \frac{1}{\sqrt{2}} \left( \begin{array}{c} 1 \\ 0 \end{array}\right) = \frac{1}{\sqrt{2}} \left( \begin{array}{c} 1 \\ -1 \end{array}\right) = \frac{1}{\sqrt{2}} \left( \left| 0 \right\rangle - \left| 1 \right\rangle \right) = \left| - \right\rangle \\ \sigma_{z} \left| - \right\rangle &= \left( \begin{array}{cc} 1 & 0 \\ 0 & -1 \end{array}\right) \frac{1}{\sqrt{2}} \left( \begin{array}{c} 1 \\ -1 \end{array}\right) = \frac{1}{\sqrt{2}} \left( \begin{array}{c} 1 \\ 1 \end{array}\right) = \frac{1}{\sqrt{2}} \left( \left| 0 \right\rangle + \left| 1 \right\rangle \right) = \left| + \right\rangle \end{align}$$

Pauli Y-gate \(\sigma_{y}\) is a bit-flip and phase-flip gate. $$\begin{align} \sigma_{y} &= i \sigma_{x} \sigma_{z} \\ &= i \left( \begin{array}{cc} 0 & 1 \\ 1 & 0 \end{array}\right) \left( \begin{array}{cc} 1 & 0 \\ 0 & -1 \end{array}\right) \\ &= \left( \begin{array}{cc} 0 & -i \\ i & 0 \end{array}\right) \end{align}$$

\( \sigma_{x} \), \( \sigma_{y} \), and \( \sigma_{z} \) are also called Pauli matrices and a square of a Pauli matrix is an identity matrix (Involutory). $$ I^{2} = \sigma_{x}^{2} = \sigma_{y}^{2} = \sigma_{z}^{2} = I $$

\( \sigma_{x} \), \( \sigma_{y} \), \( \sigma_{z} \), and \(I\) form a basis of \(2 \times 2\) matrices. So, any \(1\)-qubit rotation can be written as a linear combination of these matrices.

Please note that on the Bloch Sphere (Figure 1):

  • Pauli X-gate \(\sigma_{x}\) makes a rotation \(\left(\theta\right)\) around \(x\)-axis by \(\pi\),
  • Pauli Z-gate \(\sigma_{z}\) makes a rotation \(\left(\varphi\right)\) around \(z\)-axis by \(\pi\).
