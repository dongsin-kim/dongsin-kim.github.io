[Intro. to QC & QH] 8. Quantum Circuits - Multipartite Quantum States

Using multiple quantum states to describe quantum states on multiple qubits

Quantum Circuits - Multipartite Quantum States

We use tensor products to describe multiple quantum states on multiple qubits: $$\begin{align} \left| a \right\rangle \otimes \left| b \right\rangle = \left( \begin{array}{c} a_{1} \\ a_{2} \end{array}\right) \otimes \left( \begin{array}{c} b_{1} \\ b_{2} \end{array}\right) = \left( \begin{array}{c} a_{1} \left( \begin{array}{c} b_{1} \\ b_{2} \end{array}\right) \\ a_{2} \left( \begin{array}{c} b_{1} \\ b_{2} \end{array}\right) \end{array}\right) = \left( \begin{array}{c} a_{1}b_{1} \\ a_{1}b_{2} \\ a_{2}b_{1} \\ a_{2}b_{2} \end{array}\right) \end{align}$$

Example: if System \(A\) is in state \(\left| 1 \right\rangle_{A}\) and System \(B\) is in state \(\left| 0 \right\rangle_{B}\), the total (bipartite) state is $$ \left| 10 \right\rangle_{AB} := \left| 1 \right\rangle_{A} \otimes \left| 0 \right\rangle_{B} = \left( \begin{array}{c} 0 \\ 1 \end{array}\right) \otimes \left( \begin{array}{c} 1 \\ 0 \end{array}\right) = \left( \begin{array}{c} 0 \\ 0 \\ 1 \\ 0 \end{array}\right). $$

Remark: the states of this form are called uncorrelated, but there are also bipartite states that cannot be written as \( \left| \psi \right\rangle_{A} \otimes \left| \varphi \right\rangle_{B}. \) Then, we call these states as correlated and sometimes even entangled (which means very strong correlation), e.g., $$ \left| \psi^{00} \right\rangle_{AB} = \frac{1}{\sqrt{2}} \left( \left| 00 \right\rangle_{AB} + \left| 11 \right\rangle_{AB} \right) = \frac{1}{\sqrt{2}} \left[ \left( \begin{array}{c} 1 \\ 0 \\ 0 \\ 0 \end{array}\right) + \left( \begin{array}{c} 0 \\ 0 \\ 0 \\ 1 \end{array}\right) \right] = \frac{1}{\sqrt{2}} \left( \begin{array}{c} 1 \\ 0 \\ 0 \\ 1 \end{array}\right) $$ a so-called Bell state, used for teleportation, cryptographu, Bell tests, etc.
